Transform Your Space with Our Hardwood and Vinyl Flooring Services

Hardwood Installation

If you're looking to add natural beauty and value to your space, hardwood flooring installation is an excellent choice. At Phenomenal Floors, we offer a wide range of hardwood options to fit your style and budget, including oak, maple, hickory, and more.

    Pros:

  • Adds timeless beauty and value to your space
  • Durable and long-lasting, with proper maintenance
  • Can be sanded and refinished multiple times to allow for changes in stain color or finish

    Cons:

  • More expensive than other flooring options
  • Can be susceptible to scratching and denting, especially in high-traffic areas
  • Can expand and contract with changes in temperature and humidity, leading to gaps or warping

Vinyl Installation

For a more budget-friendly flooring option that still offers durability and style, vinyl flooring installation may be the right choice for you. At Phenomenal Floors, we offer a range of vinyl flooring options, including luxury vinyl tile (LVT) and luxury vinyl plank (LVP).

    Pros:

  • Affordable and budget-friendly
  • Resistant to moisture and spills, making it ideal for kitchens and bathrooms
  • Low maintenance and easy to clean

    Cons:

  • May not add as much value to your home or business as hardwood flooring
  • Can be susceptible to fading or discoloration with prolonged exposure to sunlight
  • May be less durable than hardwood flooring in the long run, especially in high-traffic areas

Hardwood Refinishing

If you already have hardwood flooring installed but it's starting to look dull or worn, hardwood refinishing may be the solution. At Phenomenal Floors, we offer hardwood refinishing services that can renew the look of your existing floors. Our refinishing process involves sanding down the top layer of the flooring and applying a new stain and finish.

    Pros:

  • Can renew the look of your existing floors
  • More affordable than installing new hardwood flooring
  • Can allow for changes in stain color or finish

    Cons:

  • May not be possible if your hardwood floors have too much damage or wear
  • Can be messy and time-consuming
  • May require you to vacate your home or business during the refinishing process
